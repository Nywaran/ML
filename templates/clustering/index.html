Clustering — K-Means

Grouping customers by age, income and spending using unsupervised learning.

What is Clustering?

Clustering is an unsupervised learning technique that groups data points based on their similarity, without using predefined labels. K-Means is one of the most popular clustering algorithms — it divides the data into K groups by minimizing the distance between each point and the center of its cluster.

Dataset

The dataset contains 12 customers with three variables: age, monthly income, and monthly spending. The K-Means model groups them into 3 clusters based on their similarities.
