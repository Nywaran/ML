Linear Regression — Application

Predicting the closing price of gold using real market data.

Problem Description

This application uses a real dataset of gold futures to predict the closing price of gold based on historical market data. Gold is one of the most traded financial assets in the world, and being able to predict its price is valuable for investors and financial analysts.

Dataset

The dataset contains 1,169 daily records of gold futures from 2021 to 2024, including opening price, highest and lowest price of the day, trading volume, and technical indicators such as moving averages and RSI. The data was obtained from Kaggle.

Variables Used

Independent variables (inputs): opening price, highest price of the day, lowest price of the day, and trading volume.
Dependent variable (output): closing price of gold (USD).

Data Preparation

The dataset was loaded using the pandas library. The target column close was separated from the input features. The data was then split into 80% for training and 20% for testing using train_test_split with a fixed random state of 42 to ensure reproducibility. Finally, StandardScaler was applied to normalize all input variables to the same scale, which improves the model's performance and stability.

Regression Graph

The graph below shows the relationship between the real closing prices and the values predicted by the model on the test set. Each point represents one day. The closer the points are to the dashed line, the more accurate the model's predictions are.

Each white dot in the graph represents one day from the test dataset. The horizontal axis shows the real closing price of gold that day, while the vertical axis shows what the model predicted. The dashed line represents a perfect prediction — if a dot lands exactly on it, the model got it right. As you can see, almost all dots are very close to that line, which means the model is predicting the gold closing price with very high accuracy. The R² score of 0.9997 confirms this — the model explains 99.97% of the price variation, making it extremely reliable for this dataset.

Interpretation of Results

The model achieved an R² score of 0.9997, meaning it explains 99.97% of the variation in gold closing prices based on the input variables. This indicates an extremely strong fit, which makes sense given that the opening, high, and low prices of the day are very closely related to the closing price in financial markets.
