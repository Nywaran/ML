K-Means — Manual Exercise

Step-by-step simulation of K-Means clustering with 3 iterations using a real student dataset.

Dataset Description

The dataset contains 100 student records with two numerical features: Study Hours (hours studied per week, range 1.1 - 10.0) and Grade (final grade on a scale of 1.0 to 5.0). The goal is to group students into 3 clusters based on their study habits and academic performance.

Interpretation of Results

The variance decreased from 93.64 in iteration 1 to 89.28 in iteration 2, and remained the same in iteration 3. This means the algorithm converged after iteration 2 — the centroids stabilized and cluster assignments no longer changed. The final three clusters represent: low-performance students (few study hours, low grades), medium-performance students, and high-performance students (many study hours, high grades).
