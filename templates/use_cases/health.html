Use Case 1 — Health

How Machine Learning is transforming early disease detection.

Problem Description

Machine Learning is used in healthcare to detect diseases early through the analysis of medical images such as X-rays and MRIs. Models trained on thousands of images can identify patterns that are difficult for the human eye to detect, helping doctors make faster and more accurate diagnoses.

Type of Machine Learning

Supervised Learning — the model is trained with labeled images (disease / no disease) to learn to classify new cases.

Input Data

Medical images such as X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans, along with patient history and demographic data.

Expected Output

A classification indicating whether the patient has a disease, along with a probability score and the affected area highlighted in the image.

Real-World Relevance

Early detection of diseases like cancer significantly increases survival rates. ML models can analyze thousands of images per day, reducing diagnosis time and human error.

Real-World Examples

Google's DeepMind developed an AI system capable of detecting over 50 eye diseases from retinal scans with accuracy comparable to expert doctors. IBM Watson Oncology analyzes medical literature and patient records to recommend personalized cancer treatments. Zebra Medical Vision uses ML to read medical imaging and flag abnormalities in X-rays and CT scans.

Advantages

Faster diagnosis, available 24/7, consistent results without fatigue, capable of processing thousands of cases simultaneously.

Disadvantages

Requires large amounts of labeled medical data, difficult to explain decisions to patients, risk of bias if training data is not diverse.

Challenges and Limitations

Patient data privacy regulations, integration with existing hospital systems, gaining trust from medical professionals, and ensuring models work across different demographics.
